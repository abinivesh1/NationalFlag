Flag

The national flag of India , also known as the Tiranga , consists of three horizontal stripes of equal width. The top stripe is saffron, the middle stripe is white, and the bottom stripe is green. In the center of the white stripe is a navy blue wheel with 24 spokes, called the Ashoka Chakra. The saffron stripe represents courage and sacrifice, the white stripe represents peace and truth, and the green stripe represents faith and fertility. The Ashoka Chakra represents the wheel of law and justice. The national flag of India was adopted on July 22, 1947, just before India's independence from British rule. AIDNI
